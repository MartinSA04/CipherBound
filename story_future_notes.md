# Future Story Notes

This file stores future-facing narrative decisions that are useful for writing and implementation planning, but are not fully canon until they appear in game.

Unless a point is also reflected in `story_current_game.md`, treat it as planned direction rather than already-playable fact.

## Refined Mystery Frame

- Daemons are widely studied, trained, and categorized.
- What remains hidden is not how to use them, but what they truly are.
- The deeper answer points toward an ancient symbolic system, referred to here as `the Cipher`.
- The title `CipherBound` reflects the idea that Daemons may be living forms bound to patterns in that system.

## Working World Truth

The current best direction is:

- human science has learned to classify Daemons by type, behavior, and battle properties
- Bart Iver and the player's father discovered evidence that these classifications are surface-level descriptions of something deeper
- that deeper layer is the Cipher: an old language, code, or structure embedded in reality itself
- the university shut down their research after it became too politically dangerous and too difficult to control
- Bart's confiscated work was divided into sealed fragments and distributed through university channels

This structure gives the game a strong long-term loop: each faculty can guard knowledge, not just progression.

## Character Direction

### Player

- A first-year student who begins by following curiosity and ends up inheriting responsibility.
- Their personal story should move from "I want to know" to "I need to decide what should be done with the truth."

### Prof. Bart Iver

- Eccentric genius and former professor.
- Former collaborator of the player's father.
- Fired because he kept pushing past the line the university wanted left alone.
- Fundamentally correct about the existence of a larger truth, but not always correct about how to pursue it.
- He should remain sympathetic without becoming fully trustworthy.

### Rival

- The rival works best as someone searching for an older brother who disappeared while connected to one of the university's sealed research efforts.
- This gives them a reason to distrust Bart on sight.
- They should push against the player's willingness to keep following him.
- Their arc can evolve from suspicion, to unwilling cooperation, to a serious ideological split.

### Player's Father

- Believed dead, missing, or otherwise lost to the public record.
- Was deeply involved in Bart's research.
- Should be revealed not just as a victim, but as someone who made difficult choices of his own.
- His absence should feel like both a wound and an unfinished argument.

## First Faculty Decisions

To make the story more coherent, the following details are recommended:

- The first badge should be a real credential, not just victory flavor text.
- A good working name is `Foundations Badge`.
- The first recovered research item should not be the whole truth, but a meaningful first fragment.
- A good working name is `Concordance Index`, a sealed index page from Bart's confiscated `Daemon Concordance`.
- Marius Thaule should not be a true believer in the conspiracy so much as a cautious gatekeeper who thinks Bart is dangerous.

## Recommended Chapter Rhythm

Each major story chapter should ideally provide all of the following:

- one public problem the player is told about
- one private truth hidden under it
- one major battle challenge
- one recovered fragment of research
- one new personal complication involving Bart, the rival, or the player's father
- one new route or institution unlocked afterward

## Best Immediate Continuation

The strongest next step after the first faculty is:

1. give the player the Foundations Badge and the Concordance Index
2. introduce the rival as someone personally harmed by the same buried research
3. return the player to Bart's lab for the first substantial reveal
4. use the badge to unlock restricted academy archive access
5. point the player toward the next faculty or research site

This sequence is expanded in [story_post_first_faculty.md](story_post_first_faculty.md).

## Open Questions Worth Keeping Alive

- Did Bart manipulate the missing schedule, or merely exploit the moment?
- Why was Bart's old research area converted into student housing?
- What exactly happened to the player's father?
- What happened to the rival's brother?
- Is the university protecting the world, protecting itself, or both?
- Are Daemons living creatures, living equations, or a category that breaks both ideas?
